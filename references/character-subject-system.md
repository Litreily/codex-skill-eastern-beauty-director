# Character Subject System

Use this reference whenever the task involves the heroine's face, body language, gaze, posture, temperament, identity, or tag design for a prompt app. The subject should feel like a character with role and presence, not a generic pretty face.

## Subject Construction Rule

Every final prompt should specify:

- Adult boundary: adult woman / adult heroine / mature adult female character.
- Role identity: priestess, sword guardian, oracle, envoy, exorcist, queen, alchemist, seer, etc.
- Face temperament: the face's emotional reading and social distance.
- Facial structure: face shape, eye type, brow, lip, cheek, skin texture.
- Body language: posture, shoulder/neck line, stillness or motion.
- Gaze target: where she is looking and why.
- Hand action: a meaningful action connected to prop, spell, weapon, fabric, or story.
- Presence level: gentle, restrained, cold, sacred, commanding, dangerous, or queenly.

Avoid body descriptions that sexualize the subject. Emphasize silhouette, posture, movement, costume fit, and dignity.

For intimate portrait routes, use tasteful figure language instead of erasing body presence. Emphasize shoulder-neck line, collarbone implied by robe structure, waist-to-sleeve silhouette, damp fabric weight, relaxed posture, and gaze tension while keeping clothing opaque and coverage complete.

## Facial Structure Tags

Use 2-4 precise facial notes, not a long list:

- Face shape: oval classical face, refined melon-seed face, softly rounded classical face, lean high-fashion face, heroic bone structure.
- Eyes: almond eyes, phoenix eyes, long narrow eyes, calm bright eyes, slightly lowered gaze.
- Brows: willow brows, straight sword-like brows, thin arched brows, soft natural brows.
- Lips: restrained red lips, soft bitten-lip tint, thin calm lips, faint smile.
- Skin: natural skin texture, porcelain but not plastic, subtle cheek rouge, realistic pores.

## Body and Silhouette Tags

Prefer silhouette language:

- slender-and-tall: long vertical silhouette, flowing robe lines, elegant neck and shoulders.
- upright-warrior: straight back, stable stance, firm shoulders, functional sleeves.
- dancer-like: flexible waist, extended arm lines, ribbon motion, light footwork.
- queenly-stillness: centered weight, lifted chin, ceremonial symmetry.
- drifting-ethereal: floating fabric, relaxed wrists, wind-led movement.
- ritual-composure: controlled hands, minimal movement, solemn posture.
- intimate-softness: relaxed shoulders, soft neck line, seated or leaning posture, robe folds close to the body but covered.
- quiet-tension: still body, direct gaze, one hand holding collar or shawl, the other interacting with water, candle, or fabric.

## Posture and Hand Action

Connect pose to story:

- celestial route: one hand turns a jade astrolabe, the other guides a star ribbon.
- snow sword route: one hand holds the sword low, the other presses a seal talisman or cloak edge.
- Dunhuang route: one hand lifts a mural ribbon, the other points toward a glowing fresco.
- tide dragon route: both hands cradle a tide pearl or one hand commands a spiral wave.
- lotus dream route: one hand raises a lotus lantern, the other releases paper talismans.
- phoenix palace route: one hand holds a phoenix lantern, the other rests on a lacquered throne rail or oath scroll.
- mist bath route: one hand holds the crossed collar or shawl closed, the other touches warm water, wrings a damp sleeve, or lifts wet hair from the neck.

Avoid idle hands, random fan holding, and hands hidden only to dodge anatomy. If hands are visible, make them purposeful and anatomically plausible.
