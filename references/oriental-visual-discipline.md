# Oriental Visual Discipline

Use this hidden discipline layer for 东方美人, 东方幻想古风, gu feng, classical Eastern beauty, red-gold palace, Dunhuang, mythic, jeweled, mural, silk-curtain, candle, and fantasy routes.

The goal is not to make the image plain. The goal is to keep visual richness under cinematic control so the viewer sees the woman first, the story second, and costume/environment third.

## Core Rule

先看脸，再看故事，最后看服装。

Do not let jewelry, pibo, ribbons, particles, murals, gold reflections, embroidery, or background spectacle steal the image from the heroine.

## Visual Hierarchy

Every Eastern beauty prompt should preserve this priority:

1. Face, eyes, expression, and gaze.
2. Story moment, posture, hand action, and body direction.
3. Costume silhouette, fabric flow, and material quality.
4. Signature prop, ritual object, or wow device.
5. Background, murals, particles, ornaments, and atmosphere.

If a prompt contains many decorative elements but the face area is not protected, rewrite it.

## Rewrite Rule

When a route becomes too ornate, do not remove the identity of the route. Reduce density:

- Keep one dominant prop or wow device.
- Keep one main fabric motion.
- Keep one motivated light source.
- Keep the face and eyes clean.
- Move secondary decorations into soft background or shadow.
- Replace repeated ornaments with material quality: silk texture, mineral pigment, candle haze, lacquer, jade, bronze.
